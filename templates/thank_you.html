The purpose of this study was to examine the effect of different explanations shown alongside recommended news articles. These explanations were designed to address different reasons why people sometimes avoid news (for example because they don't trust the news or because it is too negative and overwhelming). We were interested in whether showing such explanations changes people's interaction with news articles about topics they would usually avoid.

In each round of this study, you were shown a main article from your selected favourite topic, and two recommended articles: one from your favourite topic and one from your least favourite topic. One round focused on so-called hard-news topics (e.g., politics or finance) and the other round focused on soft-news topics (e.g., lifestyle or entertainment).
